I pretend not to vindicate either the means I used to obtain it, or the action of breaking the seal; but I was impelled, by an impetuous curiosity, to discover the terms upon which you wrote to him. “The letter, however, was wholly unintelligible to me, and the perusal of it only added to my perplexity. “A tame suspense I was not born to endure, and I determined to clear my doubts at all hazards and events. “I answered it, therefore, in Orville’s name. “The views which I am now going to acknowledge, must, infallibly, incur your displeasure;-yet I scorn all palliation. “Briefly, then, I concealed
